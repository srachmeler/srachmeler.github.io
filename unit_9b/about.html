Why

We live with a lot of unnecessary noise. I want to help change that by working towards a world of fewer, better things.

Your brand has a message. The purpose of design is to effectively communicate it. To do this, keep what supports your message and edit the rest.

Clutter

noun
1. A state or condition of confusion;
2. Confused noise, clatter;
3. Mess, disorder, jumble.

Visual clutter creates actual clutter

A visually cluttered page, as stated above, is noisy and tells your audience you are unsure of what it is that you're trying to say. This uncertainty leaves room for several rounds of avoidable adjustments, which can lead to actual clutter in your office.

There's no such thing as a confused audience

If you confuse your audience, you will no longer have one. Eliminate the noise. A strong design is one that delivers a clear and digestible message, which in turn, creates a responsive and loyal audience.

What

Utlilized properly, design will create a positive experience for you and the person after you.

What makes a positive experience? Listening. You want to feel that you have been heard and that your concerns have been addressed. The same is true for your audience.

Simple is neither plain nor boring

It's also anything but easy. Simple is a ballet dancer. Hundreds of hours and trials and errors are required to create such an impactful experience. Similarly, your brand is the result of time and effort to create an experience from which others will benefit and enjoy.

Hide In Plain Sight

Your visual identity should go unnoticed because it makes so much sense that your audience cannot imagine it any other way. Don’t stand out from the crowd because you draw too much attention to yourself. Stand out because you don’t stand out.

How

Design is more than aesthetics or a final execution. Design is first a strategic approach to define and solve a problem.

The root of business strategy is military strategy. Both require understanding of motivations, clarity of purpose, and how to use these to your advantage.

The root of my strategy is organization.

Organization brings to front the good and the bad, allowing you to clearly see your strengths and the areas needing improvement.
